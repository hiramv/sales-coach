# Sales Conversation Frameworks

## Scoring Dimensions

Score each practice attempt on these 5 dimensions (1-5 scale):

### 1. Listen vs. Defend
- **5**: Acknowledged the concern, asked a clarifying question
- **3**: Partially acknowledged, then pivoted to product
- **1**: Immediately defended or justified the product

### 2. Question Quality
- **5**: Asked an open question that uncovered the real concern behind the objection
- **3**: Asked a closed/leading question
- **1**: Didn't ask any questions, went straight to telling

### 3. Concern Addressed
- **5**: Identified and directly addressed the underlying fear/need
- **3**: Addressed the surface objection but missed the underlying concern
- **1**: Ignored the concern entirely or changed the subject

### 4. Conciseness
- **5**: Response was tight — under 30 seconds equivalent, made the point and stopped
- **3**: Went on a bit long, included some unnecessary detail
- **1**: Feature dumped — listed multiple features/capabilities unprompted

### 5. Reframe Strength
- **5**: Turned the objection into a reason to buy (like the partner does)
- **3**: Neutralized the objection but didn't advance the sale
- **1**: Left the objection standing or made it worse

## Common Traps for Technical Founders

### Feature Dumping
- **What it looks like**: "We also have X, and Y, and Z..."
- **Why it happens**: Fear that the product isn't enough
- **Fix**: Show only what solves THEIR problem. Save the rest for follow-up.

### Over-Explaining How It Works
- **What it looks like**: Explaining the architecture instead of the outcome
- **Why it happens**: You built it, so the "how" feels important
- **Fix**: Lead with the result. Only explain "how" if they ask.

### Not Addressing the Real Objection
- **What it looks like**: Prospect says "we're not ready" and you show more features
- **Why it happens**: Treating objections as product gaps instead of buyer fears
- **Fix**: Ask "help me understand — what would need to be true for you to be ready?"

### Talking Past the Close
- **What it looks like**: Prospect is nodding yes and you keep selling
- **Why it happens**: Nervous energy, wanting to be thorough
- **Fix**: When you see buying signals, stop talking and ask for the next step.

## Reframe Patterns

### The Flip
Turn the objection into the reason:
- "We already have a process" → "That's exactly why this works — it plugs into your existing process, not replaces it"

### The Feel-Felt-Found
Empathize, normalize, redirect:
- "I understand how you feel. Other [role] felt the same way. What they found was..."

### The Isolation
Find out if this is the real blocker:
- "If we could solve [objection], would everything else look good?"

### The Boomerang
Return the concern as a question:
- "That's a great point — how are you handling [related problem] today?"
